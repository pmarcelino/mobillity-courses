Well done

You can now take a finding and make it land — the right chart, the right library, refined until it is clear. From a simple bar chart to an interactive map of a city’s network, you direct the model and shape the result.

What You’ve Accomplished

Look at how far the toolkit has grown in a single module.

  • You choose charts by the question, not by habit. You match question shape to chart family and use the LLM to prototype options, then pick the one that communicates — knowing that more than one can be right.
  • You generate static and statistical charts. You direct the LLM to produce publication-ready matplotlib figures and turn to seaborn to expose distributions and correlations that summary numbers conceal.
  • You build interactive and geographic views. You add hover, zoom, and filtering with plotly, and you put spatial mobility data on a map to reveal coverage, clusters, and gaps.
  • You iterate to a polished result. You run the generate, see, describe, regenerate loop fluently — feeding back errors, adding constraints, describing the visual gap — and you know when to iterate versus start fresh.

Real-World Impact

A stakeholder rarely reads your dataset; they read your chart. Turning a province-level trip pattern into a clear map, or a day of station usage into a readable time-series, is how analytical work becomes a decision someone can act on.

Key insight

A chart can be polished and still be wrong — a misleading scale, an invented trend, a number that does not match the data underneath it. Looking finished is not the same as being correct.

What’s Next: Evaluating Outputs

That last point is the whole next module. You will learn to assume an output is wrong until you have checked it — reading code logic, spot-checking numbers, and directing the LLM to verify its own work — so the charts you ship are ones you can defend.

Before You Continue

Make sure the core moves feel solid:

  • Can you use the LLM to prototype and compare chart options for a single question?
  • Do you know when to reach for seaborn instead of a plain bar chart?
  • Can you decide when a question calls for an interactive chart or a map?
  • Can you run the generate-see-describe-regenerate loop and tell iterating from starting over?
