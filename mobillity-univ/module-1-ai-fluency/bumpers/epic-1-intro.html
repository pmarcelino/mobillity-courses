Welcome to the Module

You use ChatGPT, Claude, or a similar tool every week — for coursework, research, quick lookups. Casual familiarity, though, is not the same as fluency, and fluency is what you need before you let a model touch a real mobility dataset.

This module pulls apart what an LLM is actually doing when it answers you, and what it cannot do no matter how confident the response sounds. By the end, you will see why pasting a GTFS feed into a chat window is a brittle way to do analysis.

What You’ll Learn

Across seven short lectures, you will build a working mental model of how large language models behave in data work.

  • How LLMs generate text. Why these models predict the next piece of text rather than reason about facts, and how modern platforms add tools like code interpreters and web search.
  • The fixed workspace. What tokens are, what a context window is, and why today’s million-token windows still fall short of many real mobility datasets.
  • Quality limits inside the window. How attention drifts inside long inputs and across long conversations, and why LLMs fabricate confident-sounding details when their training coverage runs thin.
  • Where casual prompting breaks. One realistic attempt to analyze Barcelona transport data by pasting and asking, with every constraint showing up in turn.

Why AI Fluency Matters

Mobility data is exactly the kind of data that exposes LLMs. Your GTFS feed was not in the training set, the column names are domain-specific, and the questions you will ask depend on numbers a model cannot compute by predicting words.

Without fluency, it is easy to accept polished-looking answers that quietly invent stop names or contradict findings from earlier in the conversation. With fluency, you can spot those failure modes the moment they appear.

Remember

Every LLM answer is a prediction. Fluency means asking which patterns the model can predict reliably and which it cannot — before you trust the output.

Ready to Begin?

The first lecture opens with a deliberately simple question about a Lisbon bridge. The way the model answers it tells you almost everything you need to know about what comes next.
