Welcome to the Module

You have spent five modules turning messy data into findings you can defend — cleaning it, exploring it, visualising it, and checking that the numbers are right. Next week you present to a transport authority, and everything you need is already sitting in your notebook, beside the charts and the code.

So why do so many analysts spend the night before rebuilding all of it by hand in slides? This module takes the other path: structure the finding as a story, adapt it to whoever is reading, and let the LLM assemble the deck straight from your analysis.

What You’ll Learn

Across five short lectures you will turn a verified result into a presentation a stakeholder can act on.

  • Structure an analysis as a story. Arrange your findings the way Minto’s Pyramid suggests — the main point first, then the reasons that support it, then the evidence underneath — and use a one-sentence test to confirm the message is clear enough to decide on.
  • Adapt one message for different audiences. Direct the LLM to reshape a single analysis into several communications by telling it who is reading, what they need to decide, and how much detail each one expects.
  • Generate slide decks from your notebook. Have the LLM write the code that builds the presentation for you — a shareable PowerPoint, an interactive web deck, quick markdown slides, or formal academic pages — instead of dragging boxes by hand.
  • Run the whole pipeline end-to-end. Structure the narrative, draft the brief, generate the slides, review what was built, and refine over a few quick rounds — with your notebook as the single source of truth.

Why Communication Matters

A verified analysis locked in a notebook changes nothing. Decisions get made by people who never open your code — a transport director weighing a budget, a citizen committee reading plain language, a conference room expecting polish. Your work only counts once it reaches them in a form they can use, and assembling that by hand is where good analysis quietly dies.

Remember

Getting the analysis right is only half the job. The other half is telling its story in the way your audience can act on — and that half is just as much a skill you can build.

Ready to Begin?

The module starts with the cheapest move that changes everything: deciding what your analysis actually says, in a single sentence, before you build one slide.
